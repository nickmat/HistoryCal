Calendar Description

The Julian Basic calendar is the simplest possible interpretation of the calendar introduced by Julius Caesar and using the Common Era year count. It is the version generally used by astronomers. The year change is the 1st January, the year zero is included, years before year zero are given as negative. There is no attempt to allow for the irregular leap years that occured when the calendar was first introduced.

Use Case

The Julian calendar has been used throughout Europe and the European colonies since its intoduction by Julius Caesar, but in many diferent variants. In this form, some of its known uses are:

  • Scotland, from 1 Jan 1600 to 2 Sep 1752.
  • England and its overseas territories, from 1 Jan 1952 to 2 Sep 1952.
  • Astronomers, for all dates before 31 Dec 1582.
  • In HistoryCal, it is the base calendar for many variants.

Record
yearmonthday

The Record consists of three Fields named year, month, and day. There are twelve months in a year numbered 1 to 12. The number of days in a month varies between 28 and 31, numbered from 1.

The Epoch is set at year 1 month 1 day 1 (jb) = day 1721424 (jdn).

The Julian Basic Calendar consists of approximately solar year length years. There are two types of years, common year and leap year, which follow the repeating pattern of three common years then one leap year. The year is divided into twelve months with 28 to 31 days in each as shown in the table opposite.
