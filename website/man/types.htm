Calendar Types

The HistoryCal program currently deals with two broad calendar types. The simplest is the day count, where we pick a point in history and call it day one, we then count the days forward and back from there. The other, more common type, is when the days are grouped into months and years. There are other types of calendar but HistoryCal will be dealing with these in a future version.

Days may also be counted in cyclic groups, the most common of which is the seven day week. However, since these groups do not have an overal count we can not include them as calendars. They may be used along side a calendar and may even directly influence them, for instance, the Hebrew calendar where the new year is adjusted to fall on certain days of the week.

Day Counts

The only day count calender included by default is the Julian Day Number "jdn". When HistoryCal converts a date from one calander to another, it first converts the input date to the "jdn" value and then converts the "jdn" to the output calendar. This way, a new calendar type can be included just by providing these two conversion methods.

Other day count calendars are:

  • Julian Day. Similar to "jdn" but includes the time of day. JD = jdn - 0.5
  • Modified Julian Day. MJD = jdn - 2400001
  • Rata Die. RD = jdn - 1721425

Years, Months and Days

Here the months roughly correspond with the lunar cycle and the years with the solar cycle. The correspondence may be quite accurate or very approximate, but vast majority of the worlds calendars fall into this category.
